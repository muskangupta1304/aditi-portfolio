DESIGN PREMISE
01 — Overview

Making Money Math Meaningful

Most learners interact with financial mathematics every single day — yet many feel anxious or confused when it comes to managing money. The gap isn't a lack of intelligence; it's a lack of context and connection.

This course was designed to close that gap. By anchoring every concept in a real-life situation — from calculating a discount on a sale item, to understanding what an EMI truly costs — learners see the relevance of mathematics before they are asked to compute it.

Built entirely in Articulate Rise 360, the course is mobile-responsive, self-paced, and structured for young adults and beginners entering the world of independent financial decision-making.

02 — The Problem & The Design

What the Learner Needed

The Challenge

Math anxiety meets real-world stakes

Many young adults avoid engaging with financial topics because of prior negative experiences with mathematics. They understand their salary but not their tax. They buy on EMI but can't calculate the actual cost. The course needed to feel accessible and non-threatening while still being mathematically rigorous.

The Approach

Scenario first, formula second

Each module opens with a familiar, everyday situation — a shopping cart, a bank statement, a loan offer — and asks the learner to think before introducing any formal notation. Intuition is built before calculation is introduced, reducing the cognitive load and building genuine confidence throughout.

03 — Module Structure

Six Modules, One Financial Journey

The course is structured as a progressive journey — starting with how money moves through daily life, then building toward confident, independent financial decision-making. Each module is self-contained, but together they tell a cohesive story.

Module 01

Math Behind Everyday Money Decisions

Establishes the core premise — that mathematics is already embedded in every financial decision learners make daily. From splitting bills to comparing prices, this module reframes math as a tool they already use.

Module 02

Shopping Smarter: Discounts, Offers & Hidden Costs

Unpacks the mathematics of retail — percentage discounts, BOGO offers, platform fees, and hidden delivery charges. Learners practice calculating the real price vs. the displayed price before any formula is shown.

Module 03

Understanding Loans, Interest & EMIs

Demystifies borrowing — what simple and compound interest mean in practice, how EMIs are calculated, and what the true cost of a loan is over its lifetime. Real examples from phone loans and education fees are used throughout.

Module 04

Budgeting & Saving Without Stress

Introduces practical budgeting methods (50/30/20 rule, zero-based budgeting) through interactive scenarios where learners allocate a monthly salary across needs, wants, and savings — and see the results visually.

Module 05

Making Smarter Financial Choices

Presents decision-point scenarios — choosing between two loan offers, evaluating investment options, deciding when to use credit. Learners apply everything from the previous modules to make and justify a choice.

Module 06

Manage Your Financial Life

A consolidation module — bringing all six financial concepts together through a capstone scenario. Learners create a simple personal financial plan, reinforcing that managing money is a skill, not an innate talent.

04 — Design Approach

How the Course Was Built

🛒

Real-Life Scenarios

Every lesson opens with a situation learners have actually experienced — a grocery receipt, an online cart, a loan SMS — before any concept is introduced.

📐

Concept Before Formula

Formal notation appears only after the learner has built intuition. The sequence is always: experience → understand → calculate, never the reverse.

🧠

Interactive Decision Points

Learners are asked to make choices — not just answer questions. Branching scenarios mirror how real financial decisions feel: ambiguous, contextual, and consequential.

📊

Visual Math Breakdowns

Charts, budget sliders, and annotated calculations make abstract numbers tangible. Visuals are designed to explain, not decorate.

📱

Mobile-First Design

Built in Rise 360 for full responsiveness — learners can progress through the course from any device without losing interaction fidelity.

✅

Progressive Confidence

Each module ends with a check-in activity calibrated to be achievable — deliberately building learner self-efficacy before raising the challenge level.

What I Learned

Designing for financial literacy taught me that the biggest instructional design challenge isn't simplifying content — it's dismantling the learner's anxiety about it. The real work was sequencing confidence, not just sequencing information. Every choice about what came first was a choice about how the learner would feel by the end of that module.
